familiar

I lie where we sat
tracing the space beside me
you smelled sweet
and asked what I meant
I could not explain
how familiar you felt to me
so I stay here just
to feel close to you.
careful not to forget.

i love you

you say,

أحبّك

and I sink again.

the words
returned only to me.

set carefully
between what is,
and what never arrives.

kept there
until it aches.

waiting to be answered.
while you search
for something beyond me.

Can’t Hold On

I felt it.

but cannot

keep it.

held a place

for something

that never arrived.

waiting

for it to choose me.

to become real.

but I cannot hold on

to nothing.

still,

somehow,

I am here.

Untitled

his body arrives

before he does.

darting his eyes

across their face.

as if something there

might pull them closer.

following their breath

careful not to lose it.

he waits for the moment

it might open.

unguarded.

he almost gives,

wanting to let it show.

he leans into it.

just enough.

as the room closes in,

eyes settle where they shouldn’t.

voices shift beneath the noise.

the room tightens.

he pulls back.

still,

their breathing holds.

close enough

to feel it

pass between them.

nothing is said

but something shared.

not yet gone.

it holds there.

Habituation

i round my edges,

you soften your vowels.

we met halfway between habits,

at the threshold of breath and becoming.

we rehearse

and know the cues,

somehow natural.

for a moment

my body forgets its position.

a smile passing through

unmeasured.

a familiar unease.

without instruction,

returned to quiet.

corrected into habit.

kept until it learns not to move

every sentence

shelters a presence.

what survives grows easier to miss.

what remains goes unnoticed,

brought back in line.

survival demands I choose,

and yet

we often stay silent

not meant to be named.

The Silence Keeps Score

the floor listens before it answers

the second forgets it’s direction

the cue visible

but untouched

the minute overstays itself

while the silence keeps score

tapping a patient rhyme

ready to perform

tenuous as a verdict

not yet spoken

a quiet weight rests between them

warm only because it has been kept there

the space between them does not widen

the gap holds its shape

each moment weighted

every motion measured

and in that stillness

a quiet understanding lingers

not brief enough to belong

but what was held does not follow it out
